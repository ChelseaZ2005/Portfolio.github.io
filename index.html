It's me, Chelee!

"I'm Chelsea or you can find me as Chelee! I love pandas and cultural themed interactivity! In my free-time I am either programming, watching dramas, making music, or designing my next game<3"

I closed my eyes and hit the test button for a program I had just coded. One eye still shut, I peeked at the output panel and leaped for joy; all the test markers passed. The game I'd been developing under my studio (or cohort of game developers) had completed its testing phase. Nearly four years later, this game continues to serve a player base of over a thousand users daily, with many more options to come. Because less than 5% of our school's student body was East Asian, I realized the importance of engaging with different cultures.

My goal for starting my studio freshman year was to show there was more to East Asian culture than just K-pop and anime, and to change the negative portrayal of Asian Americans that was fueled during the COVID pandemic. For this reason, my studio placed a high emphasis on the educational component of our projects by incorporating real-life events, such as the 1898 Yellow River flood, into the game's exploration quests.

Forum-jumping between classes allowed me to assemble a cohort of like-minded modelers, builders, and user interface designers. As the programmer of the project, I worked tirelessly while giving positive reinforcement to push my team forward. Making creative ideas a reality through code became my creative outlet. It was a jubilant experience because I possessed the power to endlessly express myself within the game.

Although friends and family told us we were too young to reach such lofty goals, my community saw the development of games much differently. We envisioned it as a creative process, akin to writing a book, filming a movie, or establishing an art studio. For us, it was a tool to help others during times of stress, a gateway to reality for the handicapped, and a way to educate and build bonds when the world had been shut down. I believe that culture connects people, and my goal is to create a boundless world to do just that!
